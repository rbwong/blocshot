Participants
  • Players must be bonafide Engineering freshman students from the University of the Philippines – Diliman.
  • Each team shall consist of 3 to 5 members.
  • At least 50% of the players must belong to the same bloc.
  • Bloc handlers are not allowed to participate.
  • In case of the unknown, a maximum of 1 walk-in player is permitted. The walk-in should still be an Engineering freshman.

3 on 3 Rules

Court

The game will be played on a half court.

Teams

Each team shall consist of at least 3 members.

Format

The games will be played on a double elimination tournament format.

Beginning of the Game

A coin toss shall determine which team gets the first possession.

Scoring

The game will be played on a 1-2 point system. Every shot made inside the arc is worth 1 point. Every shot made behind the arc is worth 2 points and every successful free throw shot is worth 1 point.

Playing time

Playing time For each game, there will be two halves with an 8-minute game clock (running time) per half and a 12 second shot clock per possession. Running time means that the game clock shall not be stopped during dead ball situations and free throws otherwise asked by the official. However, running time does not apply during the last 2 minutes of the

Winner of a game

The first team to score 21 points or the team with the most points when the 16-minute game clock expires wins the game. If the score is tied at the end of regulation, the first team to score 2 points in the overtime wins the game. Overtime starts 1 minute after the end of regulation. A team shall lose the game by forfeit if the team of 3 players is not present on the playing court 10 minutes after the scheduled start of game via Philippine Standard Time.

Fouls/Free throws

A team is in a penalty situation after it has committed 5 fouls. After a team has reached 9 team fouls, any subsequent foul will be considered technical. Fouls during the act of shooting inside the arc shall be awarded 1 free throw, whilst fouls during the act of shooting behind the arc shall be awarded 2 free throws. Fouls during the act of shooting followed by a successful field goal shall be awarded 1 additional free throw. Team fouls 6, 7, 8, and 9 shall be penalized with 2 free throws. The 9th and any subsequent team foul as well as technical and unsportsmanlike fouls shall be penalized with 2 free throws and ball possession.

How the ball is played

Following each successful field goal or last free throw A player from a non-scoring team will resume the game by dribbling or passing the ball from inside the court directly underneath the basket (not from behind the end line) to a place on the court behind the arc. The defensive team is not allowed to play for the ball in the “no-charge semi-circle area” underneath the basket. Following each unsuccessful field goal or last free throw If the offensive team rebounds the ball, it may continue to attempt to score without returning the ball behind the arc. If the defensive team rebounds or steals the ball, it must return the ball behind the arc (by passing or dribbling). In the event of a jump ball situations, the defensive team shall be rewarded the ball.

Substitution

Substitutions can be done by any team during dead ball, prior to the check-ball.

Timeouts

One 30-second timeout is granted to each team. A player can call the timeout in a dead ball situation. Running time is stopped during timeouts.

Disqualification

A player committing an unsportsmanlike foul will be disqualified from the game by the organizers. Subsequent violations will result to event disqualification.

Note: Decisions made by the referees and organizers are final and irrevocable.

Shooting Stars Rules

Game Format
  • Shooting from 6 locations of increasing difficulty, teams attempt to make all 6 shots in numeric order in the fastest time.
  • The two teams that hit all six shots in the fastest times will move on to the Final round.
  • Teams will shoot in inverse order of their First round time for the Final round.
  • Six numbered shooting spots will be placed on the floor to designate each shot location.
  • Each team must choose a specific order of shooting (Player A,B,C) and follow that order thru the event (see below).
  • Each shot must be made (unlimited attempts) before the next player begins shooting in succession.
  • For Shot #6 (near half court), Player C must make the initial attempt - then all three players will rotate (in A,B,C order) until the shot is made, which will stop the clock and give the team their official time. .
  • A referee will be on-court to pass judgment on any violation of the rules.
Time Limit
  • Each team will be given up to 2:00 minutes to complete the shooting course.
  • The clock will be started by the referee and will start at 0:00 and count up.
  • The clock will be stopped by the referee’s whistle when the team makes shot #6.
  • If a team has not completed all six shots as of the 2:00 mark, the horn will sound and their score will be based upon the total number of shots completed as of 2:00.
Tie Breakers
  • In the case of a tie (two or more teams complete all shots in the same amount of time, or teams only complete the same amount of shots after 2:00), a shoot-off will be used to break the tie.
  • The clock will be reset (a time limit of 1:00 will be imposed) and the team that makes one shot from the "#6" in the shortest amount of time will, depending on the round, either advance to the Finals or be declared champions.
  • Once again each team’s three players must rotate their attempts (in any 3-player order their team decides).
  • If no team makes shot #6 in the designated 1:00 tiebreaker, it will be repeated shooting from shot #5.
SHOT ORDER, LOCATIONS AND PLAYER ROTATION
  • Shot #1 – 10 foot BANK shot from right side angle – PLAYER A
  • Shot #2 – 15 foot shot from left side angle – PLAYER B
  • Shot #3 – 3-Pt. shot from top of the arc/straight on – PLAYER C
  • Shot #4 – 18-Foot shot from right side baseline – PLAYER A
  • Shot #5 – 3-Pt. shot from left side angle – PLAYER B
  • Shot #6 – Shot from just inside half-court/straight on – PLAYER C, PLAYER A, PLAYER B, repeat...

3-Point Shootout Rules

Participants

At most 1 representative per team.

Game Format
  • Two rounds of competition – With the top two shooters from the first round advancing to the finals.
  • Three shooting stations around the 3-point line, with four orange plus one multi-colored ball each.
  • The competitor with the lower individual score from the First Round will go first in the Final Round.
  • Players may not start on or over the three-point line while shooting. A basket does not count if the line is violated.
Time Limit

Each participant has forty seconds (0:40) to shoot as many balls as he can

Tie Breakers
  • In the event of a tie in the First Round, there will be a 24-second shootout to break the tie. If there is still a tie, the process will be repeated until the tie is broken.
  • In the event of a tie in the FINAL Round, there will be a FULL 40-second shootout to break the tie. If there is still a tie, the process will be repeated until the tie is broken.
